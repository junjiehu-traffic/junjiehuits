Junjie Hu 胡俊杰

First-year Ph.D. Student
Department of Civil Engineering, Tsinghua University
Institute of Transportation Engineering and Geomatics · Advised by Prof. Ruimin Li

My research focuses on spatiotemporal traffic data analytics, traffic safety modeling, and vehicle dynamics. I develop data-driven and physics-informed methods to address fundamental problems in transportation systems, including traffic state estimation, crash risk prediction, and driving behavior analysis. My work has appeared in Chaos, Solitons & Fractals, Expert Systems with Applications, IEEE Trans. on ITS, Physica A, and Transportation Research Part C, among others.

Publications

I. Spatiotemporal Traffic Data Analytics
1

Hu, J., Hu, C., Yang, J., Bai, J., & Lee, J. J. (2024). Do traffic flow states follow Markov properties? A high-order spatiotemporal traffic state reconstruction approach for traffic prediction and imputation.

Chaos, Solitons & Fractals
doi:10.1016/j.chaos.2024.114965
Designed a high-order traffic state reconstruction method and proved the Markov property of traffic flow. Leveraged Markov matrix prior knowledge for high-accuracy traffic imputation under an auto-encoder framework.
Figure 1. Markov property-based high-order spatiotemporal traffic state reconstruction.
2

Hu, J., Bai, J., & Lee, J. (2026). A Simple Yet Efficient K-Nearest Neighbor-Based Method for High-Resolution Traffic Time-Space Diagram Imputation.

Transportation Research Record
Proposed a modified KNN framework for reconstructing Time-Space Diagrams from sparse data by matching spatiotemporal neighborhood features defined by Queen Contiguity Spatial Rule, with analytical error-propagation bounds derived via Green's function theory.
Figure 2. Efficient KNN-based high-resolution traffic time-space diagram imputation.

Hu, J., Bai, J., Yang, J., & Lee, J. (2024). Crash Risk Prediction Using Sparse Collision Data: Causal Inference and Graph Convolutional Networks Approaches.

Expert Systems with Applications
doi:10.1016/j.eswa.2024.125315
Created a crash data enhancement strategy utilizing historic spatial-temporal prior knowledge. Developed PST-CGCN — a novel method for crash risk prediction combining causal inference with graph convolutional networks.
Figure 3. PST-CGCN: crash risk prediction using causal inference and graph convolutional networks.

Hu, J., Zhang, J., Bai, J., & Lee, J. (2025). Dynamic Correlation Analysis of Urban Crashes Using Tucker-Net Based SIRS Model: A Case Study in New York City.

Journal of the Franklin Institute
doi:10.1016/j.jfranklin.2025.107946
Designed TNBSM, a data-driven and transferable model using tensor decomposition and the SIRS framework to explore dynamic crash correlations across urban zones.
Figure 4. TNBSM: Tucker-Net based SIRS model for dynamic urban crash correlation.

III. Vehicle Dynamics & Driving Behavior Analysis
8

Hu, J., & Lee, J. (2025). Car following dynamics in mixed traffic flow of autonomous and human-driven vehicles: complex networks approach.

Physica A: Statistical Mechanics and its Applications
doi:10.1016/j.physa.2025.130519
Applied coarse-grained phase space analysis and complex network techniques to vehicle-following behavior, revealing distinct dynamical characteristics between autonomous and human-driven vehicles.
Figure 8. Car-following dynamics in mixed traffic flow: a complex networks approach.
9

Hu, J., Gao, D., Lee, J., & Wang, L. (2025). Vehicle dynamics analytics based on complex network techniques: a trajectory-based visibility graph approach.

Chaos, Solitons & Fractals
doi:10.1016/j.chaos.2025.117161
Introduced an adaptive trajectory-based visibility graph (TVG) framework that transforms planar trajectory data into complex networks, featuring a tunable visibility tolerance coefficient to capture maneuver-specific spatial scales.
Figure 9. Trajectory-based visibility graph for vehicle dynamics analytics.
10

Hu, J., Gao, D., Hu, C., Zhou, H., & Lee, J. (2026). Rethinking driving style recognition: A prediction error-based driving behavior modeling.

Advanced Engineering Informatics
Revisited end-to-end driving style recognition by exploring driver behavior heterogeneity through trajectory prediction model errors, and introduced a multi-modal distribution for future trajectories conditioned on driving style.
Figure 10. Prediction error-based driving behavior modeling for driving style recognition.
11

Hu, J., Lee, J., & Wang, L. (2026). Re-examining the explanatory boundaries of car-following models through fitting errors decomposition to uncover adaptive feedback.

Reliability Engineering & System Safety
doi:10.1016/j.ress.2026.112505
Posited that car-following model residuals are a composite of structural and random errors. Utilized an Unobserved Components Model to decompose residual series from three calibrated CF models, revealing adaptive feedback mechanisms that reduced RMSE by 15.8%–61.2%.
Figure 11. Decomposition of car-following model fitting errors and adaptive feedback mechanisms.
12

Hu, J., Bai, J., & Lee, J. (2026). Meta-learning guided by physics-based residual priors: a plug-and-play training strategy for lightweight car-following models.

Transportation Research Part C: Emerging Technologies
doi:10.1016/j.trc.2026.102007
Introduced RAMR, a plug-and-play meta-learning framework that reframes CF model training as a bilevel optimization task. Validated across four datasets with three physical models and three data-driven predictors.
Figure 12. RAMR: meta-learning guided by physics-based residual priors for car-following models.
